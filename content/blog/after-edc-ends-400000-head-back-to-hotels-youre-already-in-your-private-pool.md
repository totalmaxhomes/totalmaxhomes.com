Two months from now, Las Vegas will enter its annual “time warp.”

May 15–17, 2026. When the giant owl of EDC lights up its eyes at the Las Vegas Motor Speedway , four hundred thousand people will raise their hands at the same moment, and the entire city will shift into its own rhythm. People call it:

EDC Time.

Three days and three nights, from sunset to sunrise. More than 200 DJs , seven massive stages , and thousands of tons of fireworks will light up the sky like a galaxy.

You might lie back on the grass at cosmicMEADOW , feel the bass reverberate through your chest at bassPOD , or watch the endless sea of lights from the top of the Ferris wheel. You’ll wear neon gear, exchange kandi bracelets with strangers, and sip your third cocktail beside an Art Car at three in the morning.

But seasoned EDC veterans know the truth: This is a marathon, not a sprint.

When the festival ends around 5:30 a.m. , you leave the speedway with the crowd. Your ears are still ringing, and desert dust from the night clings to your clothes.

The last thing you want to face is a crowded hotel lobby, long elevator lines, and the sound of a neighbor’s door slamming shut.

What you really need is a place where you can land immediately.

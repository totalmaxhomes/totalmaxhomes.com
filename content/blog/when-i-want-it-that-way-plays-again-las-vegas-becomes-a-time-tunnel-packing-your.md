1. Backstreet Boys: Into The Millennium

Summer 2026, Backstreet Boys return to Sphere, continuing their acclaimed residency “Into The Millennium.”

Last year, they became the first pop group in Sphere history to launch a residency. This summer, the highly praised production returns to Las Vegas for another run.

At the heart of the show is the album that defined a generation: “Millennium.”

Released in 1999, the album became a global phenomenon, selling more than 24 million copies worldwide , including over 13 million in the United States . Back then, we replayed I Want It That Way on the simplest MP3 players, quietly hummed it between classes, and copied the lyrics onto the last pages of our notebooks, imagining what it might feel like to hear it performed live one day.

More than twenty years have passed. Yet those familiar melodies never truly disappeared. Now, they return once again, brought to life inside the immersive light, sound, and scale of Sphere.

2. Why You Must Experience It at Sphere

Because this is not a regular concert. It is a time-travel immersive experience .

Sphere’s 16K wraparound LED screen completely surrounds the audience. Look up, and you are no longer staring at a stage, but at stars, galaxies, and memories that once felt impossibly far away. Combined with the Sphere Immersive Sound system, every note seems to arrive from every direction at once.

Billboard has already called it “one of the best performances of their career,” while USA Today described it as less a concert and more a sensory thrill ride.

Imagine this: The opening notes of Larger Than Life begin to play. In an instant, the entire interior of Sphere comes alive. Light floods across the massive screen, transforming the venue into a tunnel through time, carrying you from Las Vegas in 2026 back to the summer of 1999.

In that moment, you realize something.

What is more powerful than memory is a promise finally fulfilled in the future.

3. But the Story Should Not End When the Concert Does

Many visitors leave Sphere and head straight back to their hotels. They lie in bed, scroll through their phones, replay the excitement in their minds, and eventually drift off to sleep. And then the night is over.

But those who truly know how to experience Las Vegas have already changed the scrip t.

Imagine this:

The show ends. You and your friends step out of Sphere. The night is still young. Instead of returning to a hotel, you get into a car. Ten or fifteen minutes later, you arrive at a private mansion.

The gates slowly open. A Roman-style courtyard appears before you. Fountains glow softly beneath the night sky. The pool reflects the stars above. In the distance, the lights of the Strip shimmer against the desert darkness.

You change and jump into the water. Someone turns on the karaoke system, and I Want It That Way plays again. Only this time, it isn't thirty thousand strangers singing together. It's a group of friends who have known one another for decades .

There is barbecue, cocktails, and laughter. Some people lean back on lounge chairs, looking up at the sky and talking about high school memories.

Others are still singing along to songs they haven't heard in years.

Back when we listened to MP3 players and imagined what the future would be like. Twenty years later, we are still together. Just singing in a different place.

That night, you suddenly realize:

time never really left. It simply found another way to wait for you here.

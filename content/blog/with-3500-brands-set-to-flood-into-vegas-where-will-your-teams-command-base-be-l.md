In late June, Las Vegas gradually shifts into the rhythm of the summer exhibition season. One of the most important events is the Las Vegas Market (Summer Show) , taking place from July 26 to July 30. As one of North America's leading trade shows for home furnishings, décor, and gift products, it brings together approximately 3,500 brands each year. Within the exhibition calendar of Las Vegas, the show itself is only one moment in time. What truly drives the city's rhythm is often the week or two of preparation leading up to the event.

![Image](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_png/zDP7QPgHQjSj4aIBHAibDgr5zvVeljaq0ajuib0J5c0YBLwxMkb6p7R0hjbga2dT0ibpvfbQev6lPrCiaxNlsXS8jkGbIJVUMzLN8So6aR7eoMA/640?wx_fmt=png&amp;from=appmsg)

01｜July is not about tourism, but procurement

The characteristics of Las Vegas Market are easy to see:

Home furnishing brands launch new products in a concentrated manner

Global buyers gather to source products

Retailers place bulk orders in a centralized way

The exhibition lasts for 5 days

Inside the exhibition halls, the pace is intense. Outside the venues, it is no less busy. Sample reviews, brand discussions, and client meetings take place simultaneously in hotel lobbies, restaurants, and temporary meeting spaces across the city.

This week is not simply about “visiting an exhibition”, but about completing transactions .

![Image](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/sz_mmbiz_png/zDP7QPgHQjSn0etKramQKCFCD0iaB8cjjGlG3WjN54hLBx92vmbOrP35xtrBBVFbibOGKtzQqlia5bLibAPrad01lpjlFwTLMVeLmuh04qmDEVY/640?wx_fmt=png&amp;from=appmsg)

02｜Visitor flow starts two weeks earlier

In Las Vegas, there is a consistent pattern every July: hotel prices begin rising before the exhibition even starts.

Advance booth setup, business appointments, and industry networking events bring visitor traffic into the city well ahead of opening day. In the weeks leading up to major exhibitions, hotel demand typically increases significantly, business room availability declines quickly, and the number of group bookings rises noticeably.

Based on the seasonal rhythm of Las Vegas exhibitions, late July is often the beginning of one of the city's busiest visitor periods.

![Image](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/mmbiz_png/zDP7QPgHQjSMEibXHn3eBia1DkFibJcIQM3BmrX5Ss4YZkicpxnNXx73TvIJsTKs8PsFUHt5vXZghSRS4tUxFXLnCFgaRe6aA4XcUMpibTzYGeco/640?wx_fmt=png&amp;from=appmsg)

03｜The structure of this visitor group

The attendee profile of Las Vegas Market is highly concentrated:

Home furnishing brands and manufacturers

Interior designers and buyers

Retailers and distribution channels

International sourcing teams

The exhibition is open exclusively to industry professionals.

What these attendees have in common is a long stay duration, group travel, and a high volume of meetings. This is not a short individual business trip. It is often a coordinated team effort focused on completing an entire season's sourcing and procurement plan.

![Image](https://mmbiz.qpic.cn/sz_mmbiz_png/zDP7QPgHQjTZoy7Y9joZibuYX2lMVGPVpTfuHLFNfBCdKzvWZgG1icCPPw6S91R5kZoWzOuAgK4GvRqn56Lq7l1ZCKVOodtzTNJoXR0N3ec6o/640?wx_fmt=png&amp;from=appmsg)

04｜Accommodation demand is clearly upgrading

When an exhibition runs for five days, with teams traveling together and meetings taking place both during and after the show, the limitations of traditional hotels become increasingly apparent:

Rooms are spread across multiple floors, making team communication less efficient

No shared space for team debriefing or small meetings

Additional venues are required for business networking

Long stays with multiple people quickly drive up costs

Accommodation demand is no longer about “a bed”, but about a space that can support full business operations .

06｜A critical window period

Based on the seasonal pattern of the Las Vegas exhibition market, visitor traffic typically begins rising sharply in the week leading up to a major show. After July 20, hotel availability often tightens quickly, while room rates continue to climb.

For teams planning to attend Las Vegas Market:

Do not wait until mid-July to book accommodation—by then, it is no longer a price issue, but an availability issue

Broaden thinking—beyond traditional hotels, high-end short-term rental mansions are a proven alternative

Plan logistics in advance—when services such as private chefs, event coordination, and client hosting are handled by experienced professionals, your team can stay focused on the exhibition itself

July is only the beginning. After Las Vegas Market comes InfoComm, MAGIC Show, ASD, and more. The rhythm of Las Vegas exhibitions is never defined by isolated events, but by continuous overlapping waves of visitor flow .

Those who secure accommodation early have already made their move.
